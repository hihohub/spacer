To install a datatree on your web page, simply include a container element with the id DATATREE and an indented outline between the tags, here a poem by Lewis Caroll. The 'root' attribute designates the text for the root node, here 'a poem'. The 'toolbar' attribute adds buttons, here 'collapse,expand'. We have also added a style attribute of width:50%;margin-left:25%. Finally, import Jdatatree10.0.js and add another script with one line in the onload handler, JDATATREE.AutoInit();

Beware the Jabberwock, my son, The jaws that bite, the claws that catch. Beware the Jub Jub bird, and shun, The frumious bandersnatch. He took his vorpal sword in hand, Long time the manxome foe he sought. So rested he by the Tum Tum tree And stood a while in thought. And as in uffish thought he stood, The Jabberwock with eyes of flame Came flitting through the tulgey wood And burbled as it came. One two, one two, and through and through, His vorpal blade went snicker snack. He left it dead, and with its head He went calumphing back. And hast thou slain the Jabberwock? Come to my arms, my beamish boy. Oh frabjous day! Calooh callay! He chortled in his joy. Twas brillig, and the slithey tothes Did gyre and gymbol in the wabe. Jdatatree was designed for large outlines that fill the width of the page. The expanded toolset for the toolbar includes a search box, an editing menu, load and save buttons, and numbering and alphabetizing tools. The outline may be loaded from a file or embedded between the container tags. The outline may take the form of indented text or an html list. Visit github.com/hihohub/jdatatree to learn more.
